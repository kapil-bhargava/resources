üè≠ Product-Based Companies

Definition: Companies that develop, manufacture, and sell their own products or software solutions. They focus on creating innovative products for end consumers or businesses.

Examples: Google, Microsoft, Apple, Amazon, Netflix

Work Culture: Innovation-focused, long-term projects, better work-life balance, higher compensation

üõ†Ô∏è Service-Based Companies

Definition: Companies that provide services to other businesses, including consulting, software development, maintenance, and support services for client projects.

Examples: TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant

Work Culture: Client-focused, project-based work, faster career growth initially, extensive training programs

üîÑ Hybrid Companies

Definition: Companies that combine both product development and service offerings. They may have their own products while also providing services to clients.

Examples: IBM, Oracle, SAP, Adobe

Work Culture: Diverse opportunities, both product and service experience

üíº Consulting Companies

Definition: Specialized firms that provide expert advice and solutions to businesses across various domains like technology, management, and strategy.

Examples: McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, PwC

Work Culture: High-pressure environment, excellent learning opportunities, premium compensation
