What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) Explained

GST is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It replaced multiple indirect taxes like VAT, Service Tax, Excise Duty, etc., creating a unified tax system.

🎯 Key Features of GST

  • One Nation, One Tax: Unified tax system across India
  • Input Tax Credit: Avoid tax on tax (cascading effect)
  • Digital Platform: Completely online registration and filing
  • Transparency: Clear tax structure and compliance

How GST Works in India

🏛️ CGST (Central GST)

Collected by Central Government on intra-state transactions. Rate varies from 2.5% to 14%.

🏢 SGST (State GST)

Collected by State Government on intra-state transactions. Rate varies from 2.5% to 14%.

🌐 IGST (Integrated GST)

Collected by Central Government on inter-state transactions. Rate varies from 5% to 28%.

🏛️ UTGST (UT GST)

Applicable in Union Territories without legislature like Chandigarh, Daman & Diu.

GST Rate Structure

GST Rate Category Examples
0% Essential Items Fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, bread
5% Necessity Items Packaged food items, coal, medicines
12% Standard Items Computers, processed food, umbrellas
18% Most Goods & Services Soaps, toothpaste, capital goods, IT services
28% Luxury Items Cars, ACs, cigarettes, aerated drinks

⚠️ Important GST Thresholds

  • ₹20 Lakhs: Mandatory GST registration for goods (₹10 Lakhs for special category states)
  • ₹20 Lakhs: Mandatory GST registration for services
  • ₹40 Lakhs: Composition scheme limit for goods
  • ₹50 Lakhs: Composition scheme limit for services

Types of Companies in India

👤 Sole Proprietorship

Ownership: Single person
Liability: Unlimited
Registration: Not mandatory
Best for: Small businesses, freelancers

🤝 Partnership

Ownership: 2-20 partners
Liability: Unlimited
Registration: Optional
Best for: Professional services, small businesses

🏛️ LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)

Ownership: 2+ partners
Liability: Limited
Registration: Mandatory with MCA
Best for: Professional services, consultancy

🏢 Private Limited Company

Ownership: 2-200 shareholders
Liability: Limited
Registration: Mandatory with MCA
Best for: Startups, growing businesses

🌐 Public Limited Company

Ownership: 7+ shareholders
Liability: Limited
Registration: Mandatory with MCA
Best for: Large businesses, IPO plans

🏛️ One Person Company (OPC)

Ownership: Single person
Liability: Limited
Registration: Mandatory with MCA
Best for: Solo entrepreneurs

🎯 Why Choose Private Limited Company?

  • Limited Liability: Personal assets are protected
  • Credibility: Better trust with customers and vendors
  • Funding: Easier to raise capital from investors
  • Tax Benefits: Lower corporate tax rates
  • Perpetual Succession: Company continues even if directors change
  • Amazon Requirement: Preferred for e-commerce platforms

How to Get Private Limited Company

Prerequisites for Pvt Ltd Company

👥 Directors & Shareholders

  • Minimum 2 Directors (Maximum 15)
  • Minimum 2 Shareholders (Maximum 200)
  • Directors can be shareholders
  • At least 1 director must be Indian resident

💰 Capital Requirements

  • Minimum Authorized Capital: ₹1,00,000
  • Minimum Paid-up Capital: ₹1,00,000
  • No maximum limit on capital
  • Can be increased later

Step-by-Step Registration Process

Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

Obtain DSC for all directors from authorized agencies. Required for online filing with MCA.

Time: 1-2 days | Cost: ₹1,000-2,000 per DSC

Director Identification Number (DIN)

Apply for DIN for all directors through MCA portal. Unique identification for directors.

Time: 1-2 days | Cost: ₹500 per DIN

Name Reservation (RUN)

Reserve unique company name through RUN (Reserve Unique Name) service on MCA portal.

Time: 1-2 days | Cost: ₹1,000

Company Incorporation (SPICe+)

File SPICe+ form with MOA, AOA, and required documents. Includes PAN, TAN application.

Time: 10-15 days | Cost: ₹4,000-10,000

Bank Account Opening

Open current account with Certificate of Incorporation and other company documents.

Time: 3-7 days | Cost: As per bank charges

Required Documents

  • 📄 Identity Proof: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport
  • 🏠 Address Proof: Utility bills, bank statements (not older than 2 months)
  • 📸 Photographs: Passport size photos of all directors
  • 🏢 Office Address Proof: Rent agreement, NOC from owner, utility bills
  • 📋 MOA & AOA: Memorandum and Articles of Association
  • ✍️ Consent Letters: Director consent and shareholder consent

✅ Total Time & Cost

Professional Fees: ₹5,000-15,000
Total Cost: ₹12,000-30,000

GST Registration Process

When is GST Registration Mandatory?

💰 Turnover Threshold

  • Goods: ₹40 Lakhs (₹20 Lakhs for special states)
  • Services: ₹20 Lakhs
  • E-commerce: Mandatory regardless of turnover

🌐 Inter-state Supply

  • Any inter-state supply of goods/services
  • Mandatory from first transaction
  • No turnover threshold

🛒 E-commerce Operators

  • Amazon, Flipkart sellers
  • Mandatory registration
  • TCS (Tax Collected at Source) applicable

📦 Input Tax Credit

  • To claim ITC on purchases
  • Voluntary registration allowed
  • Business advantage

GST Registration Process

Visit GST Portal

Go to www.gst.gov.in and click on "Services" → "Registration" → "New Registration"

Fill Part A

Enter basic details like legal name, trade name, PAN, mobile number, email ID. Verify OTP.

Fill Part B

Complete detailed application with business details, address, bank account, authorized signatory details.

Upload Documents

Upload required documents like PAN card, address proof, bank statement, incorporation certificate.

Submit & Pay

Review application, make payment if applicable, and submit. Track application status online.

Receive GSTIN

Get 15-digit GSTIN (GST Identification Number) via email and SMS after approval.

Required Documents for GST Registration

  • 🆔 PAN Card: Of business entity and authorized signatory
  • 📄 Aadhaar Card: Of authorized signatory/proprietor
  • 🏢 Business Registration: Certificate of Incorporation, Partnership Deed, etc.
  • 🏠 Address Proof: Rent agreement, electricity bill, property documents
  • 🏦 Bank Statement: Latest 3 months bank statement
  • 📸 Photograph: Of authorized signatory
  • ✍️ Authorization Letter: If filed by CA/consultant

⚠️ Important Points

  • Processing Time: 3-7 working days
  • Validity: No expiry, but annual return filing mandatory
  • Cost: Free for most registrations
  • Cancellation: Possible if business is discontinued

Selling on Amazon - Why GST is Required

Why GST is Mandatory for Amazon Sellers

🎯 Legal Requirements

  • E-commerce Rules: GST mandatory for all e-commerce sellers
  • TCS Collection: Amazon collects 1% TCS on behalf of government
  • Input Tax Credit: Claim ITC on business purchases
  • Legal Compliance: Avoid penalties and legal issues
  • Business Credibility: Professional business setup

Amazon Seller Registration Process

Business Setup

Register your business (Pvt Ltd recommended) and obtain GST registration first.

Amazon Seller Account

Visit sellercentral.amazon.in and create seller account with business details.

Document Verification

Upload GST certificate, bank account details, PAN card, and address proof.

Product Listing

Add products with proper HSN codes, GST rates, and pricing.

Start Selling

Begin selling and ensure proper GST invoicing and compliance.

Documents Required for Amazon Seller Account

  • 📄 GST Registration Certificate: Mandatory for all sellers
  • 🆔 PAN Card: Business PAN card
  • 🏦 Bank Account: Current account in business name
  • 🏢 Business License: Trade license, FSSAI (for food), etc.
  • 🏠 Address Proof: Business address verification
  • 📋 Product Compliance: BIS, ISI marks if applicable

GST Compliance for Amazon Sellers

📊 Monthly Returns

  • GSTR-1: Outward supplies
  • GSTR-3B: Summary return
  • Due dates: 11th & 20th of next month

💰 TCS Handling

  • Amazon collects 1% TCS
  • Adjustable against GST liability
  • Claim refund if excess

📋 Invoice Requirements

  • Proper GST invoice format
  • Correct HSN codes
  • Customer GSTIN if B2B

🔍 Record Keeping

  • Maintain all transaction records
  • Purchase and sales registers
  • Input tax credit records

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong HSN Codes: Use correct HSN codes for products
  • Delayed Returns: File GST returns on time to avoid penalties
  • Incorrect Invoicing: Follow proper GST invoice format
  • TCS Mismatch: Reconcile TCS collected by Amazon

MCA.gov.in & Company Verification

What is MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs)

🏛️ MCA Overview

MCA is the government body responsible for regulating corporate affairs in India. It oversees company registration, compliance, and corporate governance.

  • Company Registration: All companies must register with MCA
  • Compliance Monitoring: Ensures companies follow regulations
  • Public Database: Maintains public records of all companies
  • Policy Making: Formulates corporate laws and policies

MCA Services & Portals

🌐 MCA21 Portal

Main portal for company registration, filing returns, and compliance. All corporate activities are managed here.

🔍 Company Search

Search and verify company details, directors, financial information, and compliance status.

📊 Data Downloads

Download company master data, annual returns, and other public documents.

📋 e-Forms

Online filing of various forms like incorporation, annual returns, changes in company details.

How to Verify Company on MCA

Visit MCA Website

Go to www.mca.gov.in and click on "MCA Services" → "View Company/LLP Master Data"

Search Company

Enter Company Name or CIN (Corporate Identification Number) to search for the company.

View Basic Details

Check company name, CIN, registration date, status, registered office address, and authorized capital.

Check Directors

View current directors, their DIN numbers, appointment dates, and designation.

Verify Compliance

Check annual return filing status, last AGM date, and compliance history.

Download Documents

Download incorporation certificate, MOA, AOA, and annual returns for detailed verification.

Key Information Available on MCA

📋 Basic Company Info

  • Company Name & CIN
  • Registration Date
  • Company Status (Active/Inactive)
  • Company Category & Sub-category

👥 Directors & KMP

  • Current Directors List
  • DIN Numbers
  • Appointment & Resignation Dates
  • Key Managerial Personnel

💰 Financial Information

  • Authorized Capital
  • Paid-up Capital
  • Annual Return Details
  • Financial Statement Filing

🏢 Registered Office

  • Complete Address
  • State & PIN Code
  • Email ID
  • Address Change History

Understanding Company Status

Status Meaning Implication
Active Company is operational and compliant Safe to do business
Strike Off Company name removed from register Cannot operate legally
Under Process of Striking Off Striking off process initiated Risky to do business
Amalgamated Merged with another company Check new entity details
Dissolved Company legally dissolved Cannot operate

⚠️ Red Flags to Watch

  • Non-filing of Returns: Check if annual returns are filed regularly
  • Frequent Director Changes: Too many director changes may indicate issues
  • Inactive Status: Company marked as inactive or under striking off
  • Address Mismatch: Registered address doesn't match actual business location
